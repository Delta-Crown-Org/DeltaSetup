Success Criterion 2.4.11: Focus Not Obscured (Minimum) - Level AA
================================================================================

IN BRIEF
--------
Goal: Don't cover the focused item.
What to do: Ensure when an item gets keyboard focus, it is at least partially visible.
Why it's important: People who can't use a mouse need to see what has keyboard focus.

SUCCESS CRITERION (SC)
----------------------
When a user interface component receives keyboard focus, at least part of the component is not hidden by author-created content.

Note: A component covered with author-created content is considered completely obscured only when the component is both:
- At least partially within the viewport, AND
- Covered by other content such that none of it is visible

INTENT
------
The intent of this success criterion is to ensure that the item receiving keyboard focus is never completely hidden. For sighted people who rely on a keyboard (or on a device that operates through the keyboard interface, such as a switch or voice input), knowing the current point of focus is critical.

The component with focus signals the interaction point on the page. Where users cannot see the item with focus, they may not know how to proceed, or may even think the system has become unresponsive.

TYPICAL TYPES OF OBSCURING CONTENT
----------------------------------
- Sticky footers
- Sticky headers
- Non-modal dialogs

As a user tabs through the page, these layers of content can hide the item receiving focus, along with its focus indicator.

COOKIE BANNER EXAMPLE
---------------------
A notification implemented as sticky content, such as a cookie banner, will fail this success criterion if it partially covers a component receiving focus.

Ways of passing:
- Making the banner modal so the user has to dismiss the banner before navigating
- Using scroll padding so the banner does not overlap other content
- Notifications that do not require user action could close on loss of focus

SEMI-OPAQUE OVERLAPS
--------------------
Another form of obscuring can occur where light boxes or other semi-opaque effects overlap the item with focus. This form of obscuring is not in scope for this success criterion.

While less than 100 percent opacity is not causing the component to be visually hidden, such semi-opaque overlaps may cause a failure of:
- 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast
- 2.4.13 Focus Appearance

The intention is that the component receiving focus should never be obscured to the point a user cannot tell which item has focus.

NOTE ON FOCUS INDICATOR
-----------------------
This criterion evaluates the focused component, rather than the focus indicator. The component itself does not include the focus indicator when checking that "at least part of the component is not hidden" - unless the focus indicator is inside the component, or focus is indicated by a change to the component itself.

BENEFITS
--------
- Sighted users who rely on a keyboard interface can see the component which gets keyboard focus
- Includes users who rely on: speech input, sip-and-puff software, on-screen keyboards, scanning software, assistive technologies, alternate keyboards
- People with limited or low vision benefit from clearly visible indication of current focus point
- People with attention limitations benefit by being able to discover where focus is located

EXAMPLES
--------
1. A page has a sticky footer. When tabbing down the page, the focused item is not at all obscured because content scrolls up to always display the item with keyboard focus using scroll padding.

2. A page has a large (30% wide) cookie approval dialog. The dialog is modal, preventing access to other controls until dismissed. Focus is not obscured because the cookie approval dialog remains on screen until selections are made.

3. A notification is implemented as a sticky header and focus is moved to the notification. The notification disappears when it loses focus, and does not obscure any other controls.

SUFFICIENT TECHNIQUES
---------------------
- C43: Using CSS scroll-padding to un-obscure content

FAILURES
--------
1. An interaction causes content to appear over the component with keyboard focus, visually covering part of the focus indicator (e.g., advertising or promotional material).

2. A page has a sticky footer. When tabbing down the page, a focused item is partially obscured because content scrolls without sufficient scroll padding.

KEY TERMS
---------
User interface component: A part of the content that is perceived by users as a single control for a distinct function.

Success Criterion 2.4.12: Focus Not Obscured (Enhanced) - Level AAA
------------------------------------------------------------------

IN BRIEF
--------
Goal: Don't cover any part of the item with focus.
What to do: Ensure when an item gets keyboard focus, it is fully visible.
Why it's important: People who can't use a mouse need to see what has keyboard focus.

SUCCESS CRITERION
-----------------
When a user interface component receives keyboard focus, no part of the component is hidden by author-created content.

This is the AAA (enhanced) version requiring FULL visibility versus partial visibility required for AA.

NOTES
-----
- Typical content that can overlap focused items: sticky footers, sticky headers, non-modal dialogs
- Cookie banners: If implemented as sticky content, will fail unless modal or using scroll padding
- Notifications that don't require user action could close on loss of focus

Benefits and examples are similar to 2.4.11 but requiring complete visibility rather than partial.
