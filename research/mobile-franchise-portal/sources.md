## Source Hierarchy

### Tier 1: Official Documentation & Standards (Highest Authority)

#### 1. WCAG 2.2 - Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
- **Source**: W3C Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI)
- **URL**: https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/
- **Type**: Official Technical Standard
- **Last Updated**: October 2025 (SC 2.5.8)
- **Credibility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)
- **Key Content**: 
  - SC 2.5.8 Target Size (Minimum): 24×24 CSS pixels
  - Mobile-specific accessibility requirements
  - Touch target spacing guidelines
  - Gesture alternatives requirements
- **Validation**: Primary source for accessibility compliance; referenced by legal frameworks globally
- **Bias Assessment**: Neutral, consensus-based standard from international working group

#### 2. Apple Human Interface Guidelines (HIG)
- **Source**: Apple Inc. - Developer Documentation
- **URL**: https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/buttons
- **Type**: Official Platform Guidelines
- **Last Updated**: December 16, 2025 (Liquid Glass updates)
- **Credibility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)
- **Key Content**:
  - 44×44 pt minimum touch targets
  - 60×60 pt for visionOS
  - Button states and feedback requirements
  - Platform-specific considerations (iOS, iPadOS, visionOS, watchOS)
- **Validation**: Official Apple documentation; iOS design standard
- **Bias Assessment**: Platform-specific but widely adopted as industry best practice

#### 3. Material Design - Accessibility Guidelines
- **Source**: Google - Material Design
- **URL**: https://m2.material.io/design/usability/accessibility.html
- **Type**: Official Design System
- **Last Updated**: 2022 (Material Design 2)
- **Credibility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)
- **Key Content**:
  - 48×48 dp touch targets
  - 8dp spacing between targets
  - Screen reader navigation (TalkBack)
  - Color contrast requirements (4.5:1 small text, 3:1 large text)
- **Validation**: Official Google guidelines; Android standard
- **Bias Assessment**: Platform-specific but industry-standard reference

#### 4. Microsoft Viva Connections Documentation
- **Source**: Microsoft Learn
- **URL**: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/viva/connections/viva-connections-overview
- **Type**: Official Product Documentation
- **Last Updated**: April 16, 2025
- **Credibility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)
- **Key Content**:
  - Dashboard architecture and card design
  - Mobile vs desktop experience comparison
  - SharePoint Framework (SPFx) integration
  - Audience targeting capabilities
- **Validation**: Official Microsoft documentation for enterprise intranet solution
- **Bias Assessment**: Product-specific but comprehensive technical documentation

### Tier 2: Research Organizations (Peer-Reviewed Research)

#### 5. Nielsen Norman Group - Mobile Intranet Design
- **Source**: Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g)
- **URL**: https://www.nngroup.com/articles/mobile-intranet/
- **Author**: Jakob Nielsen
- **Date**: August 18, 2013
- **Type**: UX Research Article
- **Credibility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)
- **Research Method**:
  - Surveyed 200+ mobile intranet projects
  - In-depth case studies with 9 organizations
  - Cross-industry analysis
- **Key Content**:
  - Field staff support: 79% primary motivation
  - Budget realities: $42K insufficient for comprehensive mobile experience
  - Offline functionality requirements
  - 7 design strategies for error reduction and productivity
- **Validation**: Jakob Nielsen is widely recognized UX authority; NN/g research cited extensively in academic and industry literature
- **Bias Assessment**: Research-based, neutral recommendations
- **Limitations**: Data from 2013; mobile landscape has evolved but core principles remain relevant

#### 6. Baymard Institute - Mobile UX Research
- **Source**: Baymard Institute
- **URL**: https://baymard.com/blog/collections/mobile
- **Type**: Large-Scale Usability Research
- **Last Updated**: 2025 (ongoing research)
- **Credibility**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)
- **Research Method**:
  - 200,000+ hours of UX research
  - 52,000+ usability scores from 150+ ecommerce sites
  - 40,000+ implementation examples
  - Quantitative benchmarking approach
- **Key Content**:
  - 67% of mobile sites have mediocre-to-poor navigation
  - 76% of mobile sites don't use image thumbnails
  - 59% violate back button expectations
  - 80% don't auto-format credit card spaces
- **Validation**: Methodology documented; widely cited in UX community; commercial clients include major retailers
- **Bias Assessment**: Research organization with commercial offerings; data appears objective
- **Limitations**: E-commerce focus; some findings may need adaptation for B2B/franchise contexts

## Cross-Validation Analysis

### Consistent Findings Across Sources

| Finding | Sources Confirming | Confidence Level |
|---------|-------------------|------------------|
| Touch targets minimum 44-48 points/pixels | WCAG, Apple, Material Design | Very High |
| Tab-based navigation for mobile | NN/g, Baymard, Viva Connections | High |

| Progressive disclosure | NN/g, Baymard, Material Design | High |
| Simplified workflows for mobile | NN/g, Baymard | High |

### Contradictory Findings

None identified. All sources align on core mobile UX principles, with variations primarily in:
- Specific touch target sizes (24px minimum vs 44-48px recommended)
- Platform-specific implementations (iOS vs Android conventions)

## Gaps and Limitations

### Identified Gaps

1. **Franchise-Specific Research**: No direct research on franchise owner mobile usage patterns identified
   - *Mitigation*: Applied general field staff research from NN/g; adapted B2B findings from Baymard

2. **Viva Connections Mobile Limitations**: Limited documentation on offline capabilities
   - *Mitigation*: Referenced to SharePoint/PWA capabilities; general mobile offline patterns

3. **2025-Specific Mobile Trends**: Limited very recent (2025) mobile UX research beyond Baymard
   - *Mitigation*: Core principles from established sources remain valid; emerging patterns tracked

4. **Cross-Platform Implementation**: Limited guidance on maintaining consistency across iOS/Android
   - *Mitigation*: Referenced to React Native/Flutter best practices not covered in this research

### Limitations of This Research

1. **Time Constraints**: Rapid research conducted in single session; did not include primary user research
2. **Scope Focus**: Focused on design requirements; technical implementation details limited
3. **Device Coverage**: General smartphone guidance; specific tablet/Phablet considerations limited
4. **Regional Variations**: Primarily North American/European sources; global usage patterns not fully covered
