# Nancy Duarte — "What Is / What Could Be" Resonance Structure

## Sources: ted.com (TEDxEast Nov 2011, 3.8M+ views), Wikipedia, Duarte.com

### Background

Nancy Duarte is CEO of Duarte, Inc., a presentation design firm. She worked with Al Gore on *An Inconvenient Truth* and with TED Talks on quality improvements. Author of:
- *Slide:ology* (2008) — Elements of effective presentations
- *Resonate* (2010) — Story patterns in great presentations
- *HBR Guide to Persuasive Presentations* (2012)
- *Illuminate* (2016) — Change through speeches and stories
- *Data Story* (2019) — Data-driven storytelling

### The "Sparkline" / Resonance Structure

**Core Discovery (from TEDx talk):**
> "From the 'I have a dream' speech to Steve Jobs' iPhone launch, many great talks have a common structure that helps their message resonate with listeners."

**The Pattern — Oscillation between two states:**

```
"What Is" ←→ "What Could Be" ←→ "What Is" ←→ "What Could Be" → "New Bliss"
```

**Structure Breakdown:**

1. **Open with "What Is"** — Describe the current reality that the audience recognizes and relates to
2. **Contrast with "What Could Be"** — Paint a picture of the better future that's possible
3. **Alternate back and forth** — Create tension by moving between current pain and future possibility
4. **Build to "New Bliss"** — End with a vivid, inspiring picture of the transformed future state

### Key Principles from TED Talk Takeaways

> "Story patterns trigger real physical responses, making ideas stick in memory."

> "Presenters succeed by positioning the audience as the true hero of the journey."

> "Moving between what is and what could be fuels audience engagement and persuasion."

> "A clear call to action and vivid vision of the future inspire listeners to act."

> "Personal struggles, when shared, give presentations authenticity and universal relevance."

### Duarte's Recommendations for Presentation Design

From her recommended resources (via TED):

1. **Slidedocs concept:** Dense data should be converted to "slidedocs" — documents created in presentation software where visuals and words illustrate one clear point per page. "Use cinematic slides to present to your audience, and use slidedocs as pre-read or follow-up material."

2. **Emotional contrast is essential:** "Your presentation will be boring if it lacks emotional contrast." Incorporate contrast between current state and future state throughout.

3. **Presenting to senior executives** (from her HBR article "How to Present to Senior Executives"):
   - Senior executives are "one of the toughest crowds"
   - They need to quickly grasp relevance and impact
   - Lead with the recommendation, then support with evidence

### The Sparkline Applied to Delta Crown

For a multi-brand operations presentation:

| Beat | "What Is" (Current Pain) | "What Could Be" (Future State) |
|------|-------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | Fragmented operations across brands | Unified operational framework |
| 2 | Inconsistent processes, high overhead | Standardized efficiency, cost savings |
| 3 | Scaling requires reinventing the wheel | Plug-and-play model for new brands |
| 4 | Compliance risk from inconsistency | Built-in compliance standardization |
| **End** | | **"New Bliss"**: A scalable, profitable multi-brand empire |

### Why This Framework Works for Executive Audiences

- Creates **emotional engagement** through contrast (not just data)
- Positions executives as **heroes who can make the transformation happen**
- Provides **natural rhythm** that maintains attention over 20-30 minutes
- Makes the **call to action feel inevitable** rather than forced
