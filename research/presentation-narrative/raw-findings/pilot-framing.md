# Pilot Program Framing for Executive Buy-In

## Sources: McKinsey Digital/Operations (lighthouse methodology), BCG, Franchise industry literature, Raskin framework

### The "Lighthouse" / "Golden Child" Model

#### McKinsey's Global Lighthouse Network

McKinsey's Operations practice runs the **Global Lighthouse Network** — a community of manufacturing sites recognized by the World Economic Forum for successfully implementing Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR) technologies at scale.

**The Lighthouse Concept:**
- A "lighthouse" is a single site/implementation that serves as a **beacon** — demonstrating what's possible and illuminating the path for others
- The metaphor is deliberate: a lighthouse doesn't light up the entire ocean, it provides a **clear signal point** that guides navigation
- Lighthouses are selected because they've achieved **measurable, replicable results** — not because they're complete across the enterprise

**Key Framing Principles:**
1. The lighthouse is NOT a "pilot" (which implies testing/uncertainty)
2. The lighthouse is a **proven model** ready for replication
3. Success at the lighthouse creates the **playbook** for scaling
4. The lighthouse generates **learning** that reduces risk for subsequent rollouts

#### BCG's "Prove and Scale" Methodology

BCG uses a structured approach for digital transformation that mirrors the lighthouse concept:

1. **Prove:** Demonstrate value in a controlled, high-visibility environment
2. **Scale:** Replicate proven approaches across the organization
3. **Sustain:** Embed capabilities and continuous improvement

**The critical framing shift:** The first implementation isn't "incomplete" — it's "proven and ready to scale."

### Framing 12.5% Complete as Strategic Advantage

#### The Reframe: From "12.5% Done" to "100% of Phase 1"

**Anti-pattern (what to avoid):**
> "We've completed 12.5% of the overall implementation..."

**Strategic reframe:**
> "Phase 1 — our lighthouse implementation at Delta Crown — is operational and generating measurable results. We're now seeking approval to scale the proven model across the remaining 7 brands."

#### The Franchise/Multi-Brand Playbook

In franchise and multi-brand operations, the standard scaling narrative is:

1. **Develop the model** at a single "flagship" location
2. **Prove unit economics** and operational viability
3. **Document the playbook** (SOPs, training, systems)
4. **Scale through replication** — each new unit follows the proven blueprint

**This is exactly how every major franchise was built:**
- McDonald's: Started with ONE restaurant in San Bernardino, then ONE franchised unit in Des Plaines
- Starbucks: Perfected the model in Seattle before expanding
- Every franchise system: Proves the model with 1-3 "company-owned" locations before franchising

**The language of franchise scaling:**
- "Proven concept" (not "pilot project")
- "Unit economics validated" (not "early results")
- "Playbook ready for deployment" (not "still in development")
- "Flagship location" (not "test site")

### The "1 of 8" Narrative Structure

For Delta Crown specifically (1 brand of 8 total):

**Frame as deliberate phasing, not incompleteness:**

| Phase | Status | Narrative |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Phase 1 | ✅ Complete | Delta Crown lighthouse — model developed, validated, operational |
| Phase 2 | 🔜 Seeking approval | Scale to 3 additional brands (proving replicability) |
| Phase 3 | 📋 Planned | Full portfolio deployment (remaining 4 brands) |

**Key metrics to emphasize:**
- Time saved per process (proven at lighthouse)
- Cost reduction per unit (demonstrated at Delta Crown)
- Error rate reduction (measured and documented)
- Scalability indicators (what the architecture already supports)

### Executive Psychology: Why "Prove and Scale" Works

1. **Reduces perceived risk:** "We've already proven it works" vs. "We think this will work"
2. **Demonstrates competence:** You've already done the hard part (building the model)
3. **Creates urgency:** "The model is ready — we're leaving value on the table by not scaling"
4. **Provides an exit ramp:** If Phase 2 needs adjustment, Phase 1 is still delivering value
5. **Aligns with how executives think:** They fund proven approaches, not experiments

### Language Guide: Pilot vs. Lighthouse

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| Pilot program | Lighthouse implementation |
| Test phase | Proof of concept (completed) |
| 12.5% complete | Phase 1 validated and operational |
| Early results | Demonstrated outcomes |
| Still building | Model developed, ready to replicate |
| Remaining work | Scale-out roadmap |
| Unfinished | Foundation established |
| Partial deployment | Flagship operational |

### The "Prove → Scale → Optimize" Arc

Present the timeline as a three-act story:

**Act 1 — PROVE (Complete):** "We built and validated the Delta Crown operational model. Here are the measured results..."

**Act 2 — SCALE (Requesting Approval):** "We're now ready to deploy this proven model across the brand portfolio. Here's the plan and projected ROI..."

**Act 3 — OPTIMIZE (Future Vision):** "As we scale, the unified platform generates data and insights that make every brand stronger. This is where the compounding returns begin..."
