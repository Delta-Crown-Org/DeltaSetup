# Barbara Minto — Pyramid Principle & SCQ Framework

## Source: barbaraminto.com (Official) + Wikipedia

### The Minto Pyramid Principle®

**Origin:** Developed by Barbara Minto, the first female MBA hired by McKinsey & Company (1963). Harvard Business School graduate (one of 8 women in a class of 600).

**Core Concept:** Your thinking will be easy for a reader to grasp if you present ideas organized as a pyramid under a single point.

**How it works:**
1. Group together low-level facts seen as similar
2. Draw an insight from having seen the similarity
3. Form a new grouping of related insights
4. Continue until reaching a single pyramid of ideas at many levels, obeying logical rules, held together by a single thought

**Key Principle:** "Communicating the thinking requires only that you guide the reader down the pyramid."

**Universality:** Applies to every document type — email, 1-page memo, multi-page report, or formal slide presentation. Works for internal or external audiences, regardless of medium or language.

**Adoption:** "The Minto Pyramid Principle has become the de facto standard for all major consulting firms and professional organizations."

### The SCQ Framework™ (Situation-Complication-Question)

The SCQ Framework is Minto's unique tool for identifying the question in the reader's/audience's mind.

**Structure:**
- **S — Situation:** Establish shared context (what the audience already knows and agrees with)
- **C — Complication:** Introduce the tension, change, or problem that disrupts the situation
- **Q — Question:** The natural question that arises from the complication
- **A — Answer:** Your recommendation (the top of the pyramid)

**Note:** Often referred to as "SCR" (Situation-Complication-Resolution) in simplified form, but Minto's original framework includes the explicit Question step before the Resolution/Answer.

### MECE Principle

Minto also originated the MECE principle (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) — a grouping principle for separating a set of items into subsets. She pronounces it "meece" (rhyming with "niece").

**Application to presentations:** Ideas in any grouping must be:
- Logically the same
- In logical order
- "You can't derive an idea from a grouping unless the ideas in the grouping are logically the same, and in logical order."

### Why This Matters for Executive Presentations

The Pyramid Principle is specifically designed for executive audiences because:
1. **Top-down communication** — Lead with the answer, then support it (executives are time-poor)
2. **MECE structuring** — Ensures no gaps or overlaps in your argument
3. **SCQ intro** — Immediately establishes relevance and creates tension that demands resolution
4. **Standard at McKinsey/BCG/Bain** — Executives trained at these firms expect this structure
