# Source Credibility Assessment

## Tier 1 — Highest Reliability (Primary Research / Peer-Reviewed)

### 1. Garner, J. & Alley, M. (2013). "How the Design of Presentation Slides Affects Audience Comprehension"
- **Publication:** International Journal of Engineering Education, 29(6), 1564-1579
- **Institution:** Penn State University / Old Dominion University
- **Type:** Peer-reviewed empirical study
- **Key Finding:** Assertion-evidence slides led to statistically significant improvements in audience comprehension (p < .01) compared to traditional topic-subtopic slides with bullet points
- **Currency:** 2013 (seminal study, widely cited, findings remain current)
- **Bias Assessment:** Low — academic research funded by NSF Grant 1323230
- **URL:** https://www.assertion-evidence.com/research-papers.html

### 2. Garner, J.K., Alley, M., & Aippersbach, S. (2016). "Slide Design Affects How Much Student Presenters Learn"
- **Publication:** International Journal of Engineering Education, 32(1A), 2016
- **Institution:** Penn State University
- **Type:** Peer-reviewed empirical study
- **Key Finding:** Presenters who created assertion-evidence slides understood the content better than those using topic-subtopic approach
- **Currency:** 2016 (findings still current and replicated)
- **Bias Assessment:** Low — academic research
- **URL:** writing.engr.psu.edu/Garner_et_al_2016.pdf

### 3. Wolfe, J., Shanmugaraj, N., Reineke, J., Caton Peet, L., & Moreau, C.P. (2023). "Advancing the Knowledge Base of Effective Presentation Slide Design: Three Pilot Studies"
- **Publication:** Journal of Technical Writing and Communication (July 2023)
- **Institution:** Carnegie Mellon University
- **Type:** Peer-reviewed pilot studies
- **Key Findings:** (a) CTML principles effective for expert audiences, (b) SmartArt/visual organizers can backfire, (c) avoid verbatim reading of long text
- **Currency:** 2023 (very recent)
- **Bias Assessment:** Low — academic research

### 4. Paul, J.W. & Seniuk Cicek, J. (2021). "The Cognitive Science of PowerPoint"
- **Publication:** Proceedings of the Canadian Engineering Education Association (CEEA) — Full Peer Reviewed Papers
- **Institution:** Price Faculty of Engineering, University of Manitoba
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.24908/pceea.vi0.14872
- **Key Finding:** Inverse correlation between cognitive overload and memory; dual-channel theory, gestalt principles, and constructivism applied to slide design
- **Currency:** 2021
- **Bias Assessment:** Low — academic conference proceedings

### 5. Mayer, R.E. — Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (CTML)
- **Type:** Foundational academic theory (multiple publications, Cambridge Handbook of Multimedia Learning)
- **Key Principles:** Coherence, signaling, redundancy, spatial contiguity, temporal contiguity, segmenting, pre-training, modality, multimedia, individual differences
- **Currency:** Ongoing — 3rd edition of handbook published 2022
- **Bias Assessment:** Very low — foundational research with extensive replication

### 6. Sweller, J. — Cognitive Load Theory
- **Type:** Foundational academic theory
- **Key Principles:** Intrinsic load (complexity of material), extraneous load (poor design), germane load (learning)
- **Currency:** Ongoing — theory continues to be extended and validated
- **Bias Assessment:** Very low — foundational cognitive psychology

## Tier 2 — High Reliability (Established Authorities / Expert Practitioners)

### 7. Duarte, N. (2024). "How to Create Slides That Suit Your Superiors: 11 Tips"
- **Publication:** MIT Sloan Management Review (Column, April 1, 2024)
- **Author Credentials:** CEO of Duarte Inc., author of 6 books including DataStory, Slide:ology, Resonate
- **Key Findings:** 11 executive presentation tips including executive summary structure, skimmability, density preferences, data visualization, jargon elimination, appendix strategy
- **Currency:** April 2024 (very current)
- **Bias Assessment:** Low-Medium — Duarte runs a presentation consulting firm, but MIT Sloan provides editorial oversight. Advice is practice-based, not commercially motivated within the article.
- **URL:** https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/how-to-create-slides-that-suit-your-superiors-11-tips/

### 8. Reynolds, G. — Presentation Zen Design Tips
- **Publication:** garrreynolds.com/design-tips (ongoing, book in 3rd edition)
- **Author Credentials:** Professor, author of best-selling Presentation Zen series (3rd edition)
- **Key Findings:** 14 design principles including simplicity, Picture Superiority Effect, signal-to-noise ratio, "slideument" avoidance, Rule of Thirds, Segmenting Principle
- **Currency:** Ongoing (principles are timeless, website actively maintained)
- **Bias Assessment:** Low — academic professor and published author. Book sales are secondary to educational mission.
- **URL:** https://www.garrreynolds.com/design-tips

### 9. Alley, M. — Assertion-Evidence Approach
- **Publication:** assertion-evidence.com (Penn State Leonhard Center, NSF-funded)
- **Author Credentials:** Professor at Penn State, 1,917+ Google Scholar citations
- **Key Findings:** Assertion-evidence structure (sentence headline + visual evidence) outperforms traditional topic-phrase + bullet list design
- **Currency:** Ongoing (website and research active)
- **Bias Assessment:** Very low — NSF-funded academic resource
- **URL:** https://www.assertion-evidence.com

### 10. Minto, B. — The Pyramid Principle
- **Type:** Consulting framework (McKinsey origin)
- **Key Finding:** Top-down communication: start with the answer/recommendation, then provide supporting arguments, then detail
- **Currency:** Enduring framework, standard at McKinsey and other consulting firms
- **Bias Assessment:** Low — established framework with decades of validation

### 11. Hazeldine, M.B. (2026). "How to Create Executive Presentations That Get Results"
- **Publication:** winningpresentations.com (Updated January 2026)
- **Author Credentials:** 25 years in corporate banking at JPMorgan Chase, PwC, Royal Bank of Scotland, Commerzbank; trained 5,000+ executives
- **Key Findings:** 12-slide maximum, 60-second executive summary rule, lead with recommendation, 3 supporting points maximum, 5 presentation principles
- **Currency:** January 2026 (very current)
- **Bias Assessment:** Medium — author sells presentation training/templates, but advice is consistent with academic research and based on extensive corporate experience
- **URL:** https://winningpresentations.com/executive-presentations-guide/
