## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes findings from peer-reviewed studies, recognized presentation authorities (Nancy Duarte, Garr Reynolds, Michael Alley), and consulting frameworks (McKinsey, BCG) on four critical topics for executive presentation UX design.

### Key Finding #1: Cognitive Load & Slide Density

| Parameter | Optimal Value | Source |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| **Maximum slides** (major decision) | 12 slides + appendix | Hazeldine (2026), Duarte (2024) |
| **Maximum slides** (status update) | 6 slides | Hazeldine (2026) |
| **Supporting points per argument** | 3 maximum | Working memory research (Miller's Law); Hazeldine |
| **Total presentation time** | ≤20 minutes (15 present + 15 discuss for 30-min meeting) | Hazeldine (2026) |
| **Glance comprehension** | 3 seconds per slide | Duarte, cited in Reynolds |
| **Executive scan time per status slide** | <30 seconds | Smartsheet analysis, cited in Deckary (2026) |
| **Words per slide** | 20 words preferred over 100 | Duarte (2024) |
| **Exec summary comprehension** | 60 seconds | Hazeldine (2026) |

### Key Finding #2: Assertion-Evidence vs. Bullet Points

**Assertion-evidence slides are statistically superior** (p < .01) to traditional bullet-point slides in both audience comprehension and presenter understanding.

- Audiences understood and remembered content **significantly better** with assertion-evidence slides (Garner & Alley, 2013)
- Assertion-evidence group showed **superior comprehension, fewer misconceptions, AND lower perceived cognitive load** (Semantic Scholar meta-analysis)
- Presenters who built assertion-evidence slides **understood their own content more deeply** (Garner, Alley & Aippersbach, 2016)
- PowerPoint's SmartArt and decorative visuals can **backfire** by implying imprecise relationships (Wolfe et al., Carnegie Mellon, 2023)

### Key Finding #3: Technical Architecture for Non-Technical Executives

The McKinsey framework + Duarte's executive principles converge on these rules:

1. **Lead with business outcomes**, not technical details ("This saves $X" not "This uses SharePoint")
2. **Use the Pyramid Principle** — conclusion first, supporting evidence second
3. **Translate jargon** — eliminate or unpack all technical terms (Duarte, 2024)
4. **Use metaphors and analogies** — "Hub & Spoke" is already good; extend with everyday language
5. **Focus on what it enables**, not how it works
6. **One concept per slide** — use visual diagrams over text explanations
7. **Use the SCR framework** — Situation → Complication → Resolution

### Key Finding #4: Framing 12.5% Completion Positively

Research-backed reframing strategies for early-stage projects:

| Instead of This | Use This | Psychology |
|-----------------|----------|------------|
| "12.5% complete" | "Phase 1 of 8 complete ✓" | Milestone framing (goal gradient effect) |
| Progress bar at 12.5% | Checklist with 1/8 checked | Discrete progress feels more substantial |
| "87.5% remaining" | "Foundation phase complete, on track for Q3 delivery" | Loss aversion — focus on what's achieved |
| Single percentage | "3 of 4 Phase 1 deliverables shipped" | Granular wins over abstract percentages |
| Bare metric | "Ahead of schedule on Phase 1 milestones" | Velocity framing — emphasize pace |

## Recommendations for Delta Crown Presentation

Based on the project's current HTML/CSS/JS slide deck for Delta Crown Extensions:

### Immediate Actions (High Priority)
1. **Add an executive summary as Slide 2** — Ensure the CEO/stakeholders can grasp everything in 60 seconds
2. **Ensure every slide title is an assertion** — Not "SharePoint Architecture" but "Hub & Spoke Architecture Eliminates Per-Brand Licensing Costs"
3. **Include a clear "Ask" slide** — End with the specific decision/approval needed
4. **Limit to 12 core slides** — Move supporting detail to a backup/appendix section

### Design Validation (Medium Priority)
5. **Test the 3-second rule** — Each slide should communicate its point within 3 seconds
6. **Verify slide titles tell the story independently** — Reading only titles should make the full case
7. **Ensure high signal-to-noise ratio** — Remove decorative elements that don't serve comprehension
8. **Frame progress metrics with milestone language** — Use phase completion, not bare percentages

### Structural Alignment (Lower Priority)
9. **Apply SCR storyline** — Situation → Complication → Resolution throughout
10. **Prepare a pre-read version** — Consider Duarte's "Slidedocs" format for advance distribution
