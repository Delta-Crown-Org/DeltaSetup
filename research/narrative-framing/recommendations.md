# Project-Specific Recommendations
## Delta Crown Extensions — Executive Business Presentation

## Recommendation 1: Restructure the Ask Slide (CRITICAL)

### Current State
Slide 12 ("Executive Decision") lists 4 actions as equal priorities:
1. Approve Pilot Deployment
2. Validate Success Criteria
3. Plan the Rollout
4. Build the Team

### Problem
This violates the "one big ask" principle. Executives face decision paralysis when presented with multiple equal choices. The current framing makes it feel like you're asking them to do 4 things, when you really need them to do 1 thing.

### Recommended Change
```
BEFORE (Current):
  "Immediate Actions"
  [1] Approve Pilot Deployment
  [2] Validate Success Criteria
  [3] Plan the Rollout
  [4] Build the Team

AFTER (Recommended):
  HEADLINE: "Approve the Delta Crown Framework"
  
  ONE-LINE ASK:
  "Authorize Delta Crown as the IT standardization 
   template — the foundation for all four brands."
  
  THE DECISION:
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ □ Approve Delta Crown framework deployment   │
  │   Investment: $0 incremental licensing        │
  │   Timeline: 4 weeks                           │
  │   Support: Sui Generis MSP                    │
  │   Unlocks: Bishops, Frenchies, HTT in Q3     │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
  
  SUPPORTING NEXT STEPS (smaller, beneath):
  → Validate KPIs (Week 1)
  → Schedule brand rollout sequence (Week 1)
  → Assemble operations team (Week 1–2)
```

### Why This Works
- **One decision, not four:** Reduces cognitive load
- **Clear investment:** $0 + 4 weeks (risk feels tiny)
- **Clear return:** Unlocks 3 more brands
- **Immediate next step:** "Approval → Phase 1 begins next week"

## Recommendation 3: Make "Template 1.0" the Recurring Theme (HIGH)

### Current State
The presentation mentions "proof of concept" and "pilot" in the ask slide. These words unconsciously signal uncertainty.

### Recommended Change
Replace all instances of "pilot" and "proof of concept" with **"template"** language:

| Current Language | Recommended Language |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| "Approve Pilot Deployment" | "Approve the Template Build" |
| "Authorize Delta Crown as proof of concept" | "Authorize Delta Crown as Template 1.0" |
| "If successful, expand to other brands" | "Once the template is proven, deploy to Bishops, Frenchies, HTT" |

Add a subtle recurring visual element — perhaps a small "Template 1.0" badge on relevant slides — that reinforces the reusable nature of everything being built.

### Why This Works
- "Template" implies reusability by definition
- "1.0" implies versions (2.0, 3.0) — evolution, not experiment
- Eliminates the "what if it fails?" anxiety that "pilot" creates

## Recommendation 5: Apply SCR Structure to Opening Slides (MEDIUM)

### Current State
The opening slides (1–3) set the scene but don't follow the classic Situation-Complication-Resolution structure that McKinsey and BCG have proven is the most effective for executive audiences.

### Recommended Change
Ensure slides 1–3 follow this exact arc:

```
SLIDE 1 (TITLE): 
  Delta Crown Extensions
  "A Scalable IT Framework for Multi-Brand Operations"
  
SLIDE 2 (SITUATION — What everyone agrees on):
  "The organization operates 4+ brands. Each needs 
   professional IT: email, collaboration, identity, 
   security. We have existing M365 Business Premium 
   licenses."

SLIDE 3 (COMPLICATION — The tension):
  "Today, each brand requires separate, manual IT setup. 
   This means: duplicated effort, inconsistent security, 
   linear cost scaling, and weeks of setup per brand.
   As the portfolio grows, this becomes unsustainable."

SLIDE 4 (RESOLUTION PREVIEW — The answer, per Minto):
  "Solution: A hub-and-spoke framework built once for 
   Delta Crown, then replicated across all brands at 
   decreasing cost and increasing speed."
```

Then slides 5–11 provide the supporting evidence (the "detailed information" layer in the Minto Pyramid).

### Why This Works
- Follows the communication structure that 100% of McKinsey presentations use
- Gets to the answer fast (BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front)
- Executives can stop at slide 4 and already understand the entire proposal
- Everything after slide 4 is supporting evidence they can choose to engage with

## Closing Note on Narrative Coherence

The Delta Crown presentation is already strong. The Hub & Spoke architecture slide is excellent franchise communication. The $0 incremental cost messaging is powerful. The phased timeline is clear.

The recommendations above aren't about fixing what's broken — they're about **elevating a good presentation into a great one** by applying the storytelling frameworks that the world's best consultants and presentation designers have proven work:

- **Minto/McKinsey:** Lead with the answer. Structure top-down.
- **Duarte:** Make the audience the hero. Contrast what is with what could be.
- **Sequoia:** One clear purpose. Problem → Solution → Why now.
- **Moore:** Beachhead first, then expand systematically.

The DCE presentation has all the ingredients. These recommendations are the seasoning.
