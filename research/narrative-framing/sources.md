# Sources & Credibility Assessment

## Tier 1 — Primary / Highest Authority

### S1. Barbara Minto — *The Pyramid Principle* (Book, 3rd Edition)
- **Author:** Barbara Minto, former McKinsey consultant
- **Publisher:** Pearson, ISBN: 0273710516
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The foundational text on structured business communication. Developed at McKinsey & Company in the 1960s–70s. Taught at Harvard Business School, Stanford, and INSEAD.
- **Currency:** 3rd edition (2009); framework is timeless and still the gold standard at McKinsey, BCG, and Bain.
- **Relevance:** Provides the SCR (Situation-Complication-Resolution) and SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer) frameworks central to Topic 4.
- **Bias:** Low — academic/professional text, not vendor-driven.
- **Key Contribution:** The "lead with the answer" principle and the SCQA storytelling arc.

### S2. Nancy Duarte — *Resonate: Present Visual Stories that Transform Audiences* (Book)
- **Author:** Nancy Duarte, CEO of Duarte Inc., #1 Global Communication Guru
- **Publisher:** Wiley
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Duarte's firm has consulted for 84% of Fortune 50 companies (Apple, Microsoft, Salesforce, etc.). TED talk has 3M+ views. Course taught at Stanford Business School.
- **Currency:** Original 2010; methodology continuously updated. Duarte.com active with 2025–2026 blog posts.
- **Relevance:** Provides the audience-as-hero framework and the "what is / what could be" story arc applicable to Topics 1, 4, and 5.
- **Bias:** Low-medium — Duarte sells consulting services, but the book is peer-endorsed by Seth Godin, Daniel Pink, and Harvard Business Review.
- **Source URL:** https://www.duarte.com/resources/books/resonate/
- **Key Contribution:** Making the audience the hero; using contrast between current state and future state.

### S3. Sequoia Capital — *Writing a Business Plan*
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Sequoia is one of the world's most prestigious VC firms. This guide was used by Airbnb founders for their original pitch deck.
- **Currency:** Continuously refined; confirmed live as of April 2026.
- **Source URL:** https://sequoiacap.com/article/writing-a-business-plan/
- **Relevance:** Provides the canonical pitch structure (Purpose → Problem → Solution → Why Now → Market → Competition → Business Model → Team → Financials → Vision) relevant to Topics 2 and 5.
- **Bias:** Low — Sequoia is a consumer of pitches, not a seller.
- **Key Contribution:** The "why now?" question and the problem→solution→vision arc.

## Tier 2 — High Authority

### S4. StrategyU — *SCQA: A Framework for Defining Problems & Hypotheses*
- **Author:** Paul Millerd, former McKinsey and Boston Consulting Group
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Author has direct Big 3 consulting experience.
- **Currency:** Published June 2019; framework is timeless.
- **Source URL:** https://strategyu.co/scqa-a-framework-for-defining-problems-hypotheses/
- **Relevance:** Detailed walkthrough of SCQA framework with university case study example.
- **Bias:** Medium — StrategyU sells consulting courses, but content is pedagogical.
- **Key Contribution:** Practical SCQA example showing Situation/Complication decomposition and hypothesis development.

### S5. Untools.co — *Minto Pyramid*
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Well-curated thinking tools resource, accurately cites primary sources.
- **Currency:** Active as of April 2026.
- **Source URL:** https://untools.co/minto-pyramid/
- **Relevance:** Concise, accurate summary of the Minto Pyramid's top-down communication structure.
- **Bias:** Low — educational resource, no commercial motivation.
- **Key Contribution:** The "BLUF" (Bottom Line Up Front) principle and 3-tier structure: conclusion → key arguments → supporting details.

### S6. Geoffrey Moore — *Crossing the Chasm* (Book, 3rd Edition, 2014)
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Seminal work on technology adoption lifecycle. Moore is a partner at Wildcat Venture Partners and organizational theorist.
- **Relevance:** Provides the "beachhead strategy" framework foundational to the "land and expand" concept (Topic 2). The idea that you dominate one narrow segment before expanding to adjacent ones.
- **Bias:** Low — academic/professional text.
- **Key Contribution:** The beachhead market concept; the bowling pin strategy for sequential market capture.

### S7. McKinsey & Company — Structured Communication Methodology
- **Authority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Original source firm for the Pyramid Principle and SCR framework.
- **Note:** McKinsey.com was inaccessible during research (HTTP/2 protocol errors). Relied on secondary sources (S4, S5) that accurately represent the methodology.
- **Key Contribution:** The SCR framework as internal consulting standard.
