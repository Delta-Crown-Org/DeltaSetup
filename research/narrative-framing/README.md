# Narrative Framing in Business Presentations
## Executive Research Summary

**Research ID:** `web-puppy-f4e706`
**Date:** 2026-04-01
**Project Context:** Delta Crown Extensions — Executive Business Presentation for franchise IT infrastructure pilot

---

## Key Findings

### 1. The "Golden Child" / Flagship Narrative
**Core Insight:** Position the pilot (Delta Crown) not as an experiment, but as the **proven template** that makes every subsequent deployment faster, cheaper, and lower risk. The golden child narrative transforms a single-brand project into a strategic platform investment.

**Relevance to DCE Presentation:** The current presentation already uses this pattern (Delta Crown → Bishops → Frenchies → HTT), but can be strengthened by making the "template" value more explicit — every slide should whisper "and this works for every brand."

### 2. The "Land and Expand" Pitch Structure
**Core Insight:** Originating from enterprise SaaS strategy, "land and expand" means securing a small initial foothold (the "land"), proving value, then systematically growing scope (the "expand"). For executive presentations, the pilot IS the land — the ask should frame approval as unlocking the expansion pathway.

**Relevance to DCE Presentation:** The presentation's "Scalability Roadmap" slide (Slide 10) and phased timeline already embody this. The ask slide should make the land/expand economics explicit: "Approve one, unlock four."

### 3. Franchise Standardization / Operational Framework Presentation
**Core Insight:** Executives who care about scalability respond to three things: (1) unit economics that improve with scale, (2) governance that doesn't require per-unit attention, and (3) time-to-deploy that shrinks with each new instance. The SharePoint Hub & Spoke architecture slide already communicates this well.

### 4. Storytelling Frameworks (BAB & SCR)
**Core Insight:** Two complementary frameworks dominate executive business presentations:
- **Before/After/Bridge (BAB):** Paint the painful current state → show the desirable future → reveal your solution as the bridge
- **Situation-Complication-Resolution (SCR) / SCQA:** Establish shared context → introduce the tension → present your answer

The DCE presentation should use SCR at the macro level (entire deck arc) and BAB at the micro level (individual slides).

### 5. The "Ask Slide" Best Practices
**Core Insight:** The ask slide is the single most important slide in any executive presentation. It must be: specific, time-bound, low-friction, and framed as a gateway to greater value. The current DCE "Immediate Actions" slide (Slide 12) lists 4 actions — best practice says **lead with one clear ask**, then support with next steps.
