# Franchisee Personas and User Needs

**Research ID**: web-puppy-3e7ada  
**Date**: January 2025  
**Sources**: Nielsen Norman Group Mobile Intranet Research, SharePoint Usage Patterns, Franchise Industry Research

## Executive Summary

Franchisees are unique users - they are **independent business operators** who have invested significantly in a franchise system, but maintain autonomy in daily operations. Unlike employees who must use corporate systems, franchisees choose whether to engage with the portal based on perceived value.

## Primary Persona: The Franchise Owner-Operator

### Demographics
- **Age**: 35-55 (core), expanding to younger entrepreneurs
- **Experience**: Mix of first-time business owners and multi-unit operators
- **Tech Comfort**: Varies widely - from early adopters to reluctant users
- **Time Availability**: Extremely limited - wearing multiple hats

### Goals and Motivations

**Business Success**:
- Achieve profitability and growth
- Maintain brand standards
- Build local reputation
- Develop effective team
- Scale to multiple units

**Operational Excellence**:
- Streamline daily operations
- Reduce time on administrative tasks
- Access resources efficiently
- Stay compliant with standards
- Learn from best practices

**Franchise Relationship**:
- Feel supported by franchisor
- Connect with peer franchisees
- Stay informed about changes
- Access timely support
- Contribute to brand success

### Pain Points

**Information Access**:
> "I know there's a document about that somewhere, but I can never find it when I need it."

- Can't find current versions of documents
- Information scattered across multiple systems
- Not sure what's changed recently
- Search doesn't work well
- Mobile access is frustrating

**Time Constraints**:
> "I'm managing the floor, the books, the team, and customers. I don't have time to dig through a portal."

- Long training modules
- Complex navigation
- Too many clicks to complete tasks
- Desktop-only access
- Notifications not actionable

**Communication Fragmentation**:
> "I get information by email, text, phone calls, and sometimes the portal. It's impossible to keep track."

- Multiple communication channels
- Important updates missed
- No single source of truth
- Inconsistent messaging
- Difficult to reference later

**Training and Support**:
> "When I have a problem, I need an answer NOW, not after clicking through 10 screens."

- Support hard to find
- Training not relevant
- No just-in-time resources
- Can't get help quickly
- Solutions don't fit local context

**Technology Frustration**:
> "The portal is just another login I have to remember. It doesn't integrate with anything I use."

- Too many passwords
- Interface is confusing
- Doesn't work on my phone
- Slow performance
- Not integrated with daily tools

## Detailed Personas

### Persona 1: Sarah - The Multi-Tasking Owner

**Background**: 
- First-time franchisee, 2 years in
- Owns single location in suburban area
- Previous career in corporate marketing
- Tech-savvy but time-poor

**Daily Routine**:
- 6:00 AM: Review overnight reports, check portal for updates
- 7:00 AM: Opening duties, team coordination
- 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM: Customer service, floor management
- 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM: Administrative work (often in car between meetings)
- 3:00 PM - 7:00 PM: Afternoon/evening operations
- 8:00 PM: Quick portal check for urgent items

**Portal Usage Patterns**:
- **Desktop**: 20% (mostly at beginning/end of day)
- **Mobile**: 80% (throughout the day, on-the-go)
- **Sessions**: 5-10 per day, 2-3 minutes each
- **Peak times**: 6-7 AM, 2-3 PM, 8-9 PM

**Key Tasks**:
1. Check daily sales/operations reports
2. Access quick reference guides
3. Submit support tickets
4. Review and approve team schedules
5. Access training for new initiatives

**Technology Context**:
- **Primary device**: iPhone (always with her)
- **Secondary device**: iPad for longer reading
- **Computer**: Laptop at home, rarely used during day
- **Connectivity**: Strong WiFi at location, variable mobile

**Frustrations**:
- Documents don't display well on mobile
- Search doesn't find things she knows exist
- Too many notifications, can't tell which are urgent
- Training takes too long
- Can't easily share resources with team

**Success Scenario**:
> "I get a notification: 'New summer promotion materials available.' I tap it, preview the flyer on my phone, download the PDF, and share it to my location's group chat in 30 seconds. My team has it printed before the first customer."

### Persona 2: Mike - The Seasoned Operator

**Background**:
- Multi-unit franchisee, 15 years in system
- Owns 4 locations in metro area
- Business is well-established
- Focuses on strategy, not day-to-day

**Daily Routine**:
- 7:00 AM: Review dashboards, check all locations
- 8:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Travel between locations
- 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM: Lunch with GM, business development
- 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM: Meetings, administrative work
- 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM: Check-in with locations, review performance

**Portal Usage Patterns**:
- **Desktop**: 60% (in office or home)
- **Mobile**: 40% (between locations, quick checks)
- **Sessions**: 3-5 per day, 10-20 minutes each
- **Peak times**: 7-8 AM, 2-5 PM, 7-8 PM

**Key Tasks**:
1. Review performance dashboards across locations
2. Access strategic resources
3. Coordinate with other franchisees
4. Plan new initiatives
5. Compliance and reporting

**Technology Context**:
- **Primary device**: Laptop (in office)
- **Secondary device**: iPhone (in field)
- **Tablet**: Occasionally for board meetings
- **Connectivity**: Strong WiFi at office, good mobile

**Frustrations**:
- Can't see consolidated view across all locations
- Reports hard to customize
- No way to benchmark against other franchisees
- Support tickets take too long
- Missing visibility into peer best practices

**Success Scenario**:
> "I log in and see my dashboard with all four locations' KPIs. I drill into Location 2's customer satisfaction dip, find a case study from another franchisee who solved similar issue, and schedule a call with them. I also notice a compliance update that affects all locations and assign tasks to each GM."

### Persona 3: Alex - The Tech-Savvy Millennial

**Background**:
- Second-generation franchisee
- Recently took over family business
- Digital native, expects consumer-grade UX
- Wants to modernize operations

**Daily Routine**:
- 8:00 AM: Check social media, portal updates
- 9:00 AM: Team stand-up via video
- 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM: Flexible schedule, mix of office and field
- 7:00 PM: Community engagement online
- 9:00 PM: Review next day, set priorities

**Portal Usage Patterns**:
- **Desktop**: 40% (planning, analysis)
- **Mobile**: 60% (throughout day, notifications)
- **Sessions**: 10-15 per day, 1-5 minutes each
- **Peak times**: Throughout day, responsive to notifications

**Key Tasks**:
1. Collaborate with other franchisees
2. Share best practices
3. Access social media content
4. Quick operational lookups
5. Community engagement

**Technology Context**:
- **Primary device**: iPhone (always connected)
- **Secondary device**: MacBook Pro
- **Tablet**: iPad for content creation
- **Connectivity**: Always connected, expects instant

**Frustrations**:
- Portal feels dated compared to consumer apps
- No dark mode
- Can't customize layout
- Limited social features
- No gamification or recognition

**Success Scenario**:
> "I get a notification that my post in the community forum got 50 likes. I check it, see thoughtful comments from other franchisees, and we start a group chat about implementing one of the ideas. I also notice I'm climbing the engagement leaderboard and earned a 'Community Champion' badge."

## User Needs Hierarchy

### Critical (Must Have)
1. **Mobile access** - Work anywhere
2. **Quick task completion** - Save time
3. **Current documents** - Access right version
4. **Search that works** - Find information
5. **Fast support** - Get help when needed

### Important (Should Have)
1. **Training** - Build skills
2. **Community** - Connect with peers
3. **Dashboards** - Monitor performance
4. **Notifications** - Stay informed
5. **Offline access** - Work without connectivity

### Nice to Have (Could Have)
1. **Gamification** - Engagement
2. **Customization** - Personalize experience
3. **Social features** - Recognition
4. **Advanced analytics** - Deep insights
5. **Integration** - Connect with other tools

## Design Implications

### Mobile-First Design
- Design for mobile first, then scale up
- Minimum 44px touch targets
- Thumb-friendly navigation zones
- Swipe gestures
- Bottom-aligned primary actions

### Progressive Disclosure
- Show critical information first
- Hide details behind expandable sections
- Quick actions visible, details accessible
- Card-based layouts
- Expandable content

### Task-Focused Organization
- Group by task, not department
- Quick links to common actions
- Role-based home pages
- Personalized dashboards
- Recently used prominently displayed

### Offline Capability
- Save state during signal loss
- Cache critical documents
- Queue actions for sync
- Progressive loading
- Offline indicators

### Search-First Design
- Prominent search bar
- Type-ahead suggestions
- Recent searches
- Filter options
- Results ranked by relevance and recency

### Notification Management
- Prioritize by urgency
- Allow batch dismiss
- Actionable notifications
- Quiet hours
- Channel preferences

## Research-Based Recommendations

### From Nielsen Norman Group Mobile Intranet Research

1. **Plan for offline viewing** - Store data, save state, remember context
2. **Optimize load times** - Minimize server calls, lightweight pages
3. **Minimize data input** - Selection over typing, auto-suggestions
4. **Design workflows** - Logical task flows, reduced burden
5. **Make smart assumptions** - Pre-populate, remember selections
6. **Focus on typography** - Larger fonts, clear hierarchy
7. **Write for mobile** - Bite-sized content, progressive disclosure

### From SharePoint Best Practices

1. **Hub-and-spoke architecture** - Organize by function
2. **Audience targeting** - Role-based content visibility
3. **News aggregation** - Roll-up from associated sites
4. **Mobile app** - Native mobile experience
5. **Search optimization** - Configured search verticals
6. **Content freshness** - Governance and lifecycle
