# Executive Empathy Mapping for C-Suite Presentations
## Research Report — Delta Crown Hair Extensions Pilot Program

> **Research Date:** April 2026
> **Project Context:** Delta Crown Extensions — M365-based franchise operational framework with SharePoint Hub & Spoke architecture, automated provisioning, and brand instance deployment
> **Target Audience:** CEO, CFO, COO, CTO evaluating a pilot program before scaling

---

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes current best practices (2024–2026) from Deloitte, IBM IBV, McKinsey/Minto, Forbes Technology Council, and government program evaluation frameworks to build a complete empathy map for presenting the Delta Crown operational framework to C-suite executives.

### Key Findings

1. **CFOs are the primary gatekeepers** — 50% cite digital transformation as their #1 priority for 2026, but demand clear ROI and bounded risk (Deloitte Q4 2025 CFO Signals)
2. **Lead with the answer, not the journey** — The Pyramid Principle (Minto/McKinsey) dictates: recommendation first, 3 supporting points, evidence beneath each
3. **Executives decide in 60 seconds** — They need: What's the decision? Why now? How contained is the risk? (Approval Packet Method)
4. **90% of tech pilots fail to reach production** — The #1 killer is "pilot purgatory" from unclear business cases and missing scaling plans (McKinsey/Forbes)
5. **Franchise/multi-unit scaling demands** standardized operations, hub governance, and incremental rollout with clear kill switches

### Recommendations for Delta Crown Presentation

| Priority | Action | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| 🔴 Critical | Restructure deck to Pyramid Principle (answer-first) | Executives check out by slide 4 if building to a conclusion |
| 🔴 Critical | Add explicit "Risk Box" slide with kill switch trigger | Vague risk = assumed larger risk = delayed decision |
| 🟡 High | Frame costs as "zero incremental license cost" with total cost of ownership | CFOs think in ROI horizons, not feature lists |
| 🟡 High | Add 30-day post-approval action plan | "What happens next" must be obvious for approval |
| 🟢 Medium | Create per-role value cards (CEO/CFO/COO/CTO) | Each executive filters through different priorities |
| 🟢 Medium | Include "pilot purgatory" escape plan with scaling roadmap | Shows you understand the #1 executive fear |

## Quick-Reference: What Each Executive Cares About

### CEO — "Does this scale our competitive advantage?"
- Market position and brand differentiation
- Speed of scaling across units
- Organizational agility and talent leverage
- "Will this make us faster than competitors?"

### CFO — "Show me the ROI and bound the risk"
- Total cost of ownership vs. current state
- Payback period and break-even point
- Risk quantification with mitigation plan
- "What's the worst-case scenario and how do we exit?"

### COO — "Will this actually work in daily operations?"
- Implementation complexity and timeline
- Impact on existing workflows and team capacity
- Standardization and governance consistency
- "Can my team run this without consultants?"

### CTO — "Is this architecturally sound and future-proof?"
- Technical debt and vendor lock-in risk
- Integration with existing M365 infrastructure
- Security, compliance, and data governance
- "Does this scale technically and will Microsoft support this model?"
