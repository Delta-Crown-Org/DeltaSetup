# Executive Objections to Digital Transformation — Raw Findings
## Forbes Technology Council (20 Expert Practitioners)
**Source:** https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbestechcouncil/2024/06/28/why-digital-transformation-often-fails-and-how-to-succeed/
**Published:** June 28, 2024

## The 20 Reasons Digital Transformation Fails

### Strategy & Vision Failures
1. **No understanding of target architecture** — Organizations focus on technology but neglect customer experience impact (Margarita Repina, RepinaTech)
2. **No defined meaning or strategic alignment** — Organization fails to define what digital transformation means for its business (Geetha Kumari Kommepalli, Skillsoft)
3. **No clear 'why'** — Benefits must be shared by all stakeholders, even those whose roles become redundant (Stephen Farrugia, BAI Communications)
4. **Not grounded in CX** — Scope spirals when technology is pursued for technology's sake (Biju Nair, Assurant)

### Execution & Management Failures
5. **Team loses sight of goals** — Need data-led goals at outset with regular check-ins (David Grant, Westcon-Comstor)
6. **Too much focus on microprocesses** — Volume of details puts pressure on team members (Max Dinman, RichBrains)
7. **No strong product owner** — Various stakeholders add unnecessary features without control (Nik Froehlich, Saritasa)
8. **No MVP** — Without results in first 90 days, executives halt the project (Deepak Tiwari, Ernst & Young)
9. **No dedicated team** — Lack of structured project management (Christoffer Bouet, Contribe)
10. **Teams try to do too much or too little** — Need strategic view with focus on high-value process execution (Henry Patishman, Regula)

### People & Culture Failures
11. **Employee resistance to change** — Adaptation requires effort and benefits aren't always evident (Ilya Lashch, Lightpoint Global)
12. **Lack of needed skills** — Mistaken assumption that right people will materialize (Alicia Roach, eQ8)
13. **Requires culture shift** — Becoming a software company challenges traditional business management (Victor Shilo, EastBanc Technologies)
14. **Poor change management and communication** — "Crawl, walk, run" approach needed (Hemant Sahani, VMware)

### Technical Failures
15. **Inadequate IT infrastructure** — Existing systems can't support digital initiatives (Parul Batra, neuro42)
16. **Legacy systems get in the way** — Decades-old systems with custom code (Alfred Kahn, OvationCXM)
17. **Lack of quality, connected data** — Disconnected data sows doubt in decision-making (Ed Jennings, Quickbase)

### Resource & Investment Failures
18. **Only investment is in technology** — Must transform people, processes AND technology in tandem (Vikrant Labde, Cuelogic Technologies)
19. **Resource-intensive** — Training and internal resources must be allocated (Itzik Levy, vcita)

## Mapped to Executive Objection Categories

### Value Objections ("Is it worth it?")
- No clear ROI or strategic alignment (#2, #4, #5)
- No MVP to demonstrate value (#8)

### Execution Objections ("Will it work?")
- Team lacks skills (#12)
- Infrastructure isn't ready (#15, #16)
- Too complex (#6, #10)

### Risk Objections ("What if it goes wrong?")
- No product owner to control scope (#7)
- Legacy integration risks (#16)
- Data quality issues (#17)

### Adoption Objections ("Will people use it?")
- Resistance to change (#11, #13)
- Poor communication (#14)
- Culture shift required (#13)
- Only investing in technology, not people (#18)
